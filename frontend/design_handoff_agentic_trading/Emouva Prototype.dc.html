Quality Minus Junk {{ qmjStatus }}
Asness, Frazzini & Pedersen · 2014 · uploaded by you, Jun 11 · read in 4 min by the Partner
The gist
Stocks of "quality" companies — profitable, growing, safe, well-run — persistently outperform junk, and the market persistently underprices that quality. The premium held across 24 countries and 60 years of data.
The inversion — how does this fail?
I tried to kill it three ways. It survives two:
SURVIVESCrowding: quality got expensive post-2016, but the premium persisted net of valuation.
SURVIVESData mining: out-of-sample and cross-country results are consistent.
FAILSSharp junk rallies (2009, Apr 2020): quality lags violently for 1–2 quarters. We must be willing to look wrong briefly.
